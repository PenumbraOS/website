<p class="section-label">The Journey</p>
<h2>The Ai Pin still works.<br>The cloud around it disappeared.</h2>
</div>
</div>
<div class="why-timeline">
<div class="why-timeline-line-wrap" aria-hidden="true">
<div class="why-timeline-line why-timeline-line--track"></div>
<div class="why-timeline-line why-timeline-line--fill"></div>
</div>

<div class="why-timeline-row why-timeline-row--card-left">
<article class="why-tl-card">
<p class="why-tl-card__meta">February 28, 2025</p>
<h3 class="why-tl-card__title">The shutdown</h3>
<p class="why-tl-card__body">Humane shut down the cloud services every Ai Pin depended on. Devices that once felt like the future went dark in people's hands, with no local access and no official path forward.</p>
</article>
<div class="why-timeline-spine"><span class="why-timeline-dot"></span></div>
<aside class="why-timeline-aside">
<p class="why-timeline-aside__text">Humane built the Ai Pin around a locked cloud model. The device was tightly restricted, while core activity still flowed through Humane's servers. When those servers shut down, ownership did not matter much. The hardware could not stand on its own.</p>
</aside>
</div>

<div class="why-timeline-row why-timeline-row--card-right">
<aside class="why-timeline-aside">
<p class="why-timeline-aside__text">Even with device access through the leaked ADB certificate, ordinary apps remain boxed out of what makes the Pin useful: networking, the touchpad, hand gestures, camera flows, and the deeper cosmOS internals.</p>
</aside>
<div class="why-timeline-spine"><span class="why-timeline-dot"></span></div>
<article class="why-tl-card">
<p class="why-tl-card__meta">March 1, 2025</p>
<h3 class="why-tl-card__title">The work began</h3>
<p class="why-tl-card__body">The next morning, Adam started pulling at the thread. Not because the answer was obvious, but because the device was still there, still capable, and still worth fighting for.</p>
</article>
</div>

<div class="why-timeline-row why-timeline-row--card-left">
<article class="why-tl-card">
<p class="why-tl-card__meta">March 21, 2025</p>
<h3 class="why-tl-card__title">The first door opened</h3>
<p class="why-tl-card__body">Weeks of quiet work turned into the first real breakthrough. The Ai Pin was no longer a sealed object. It could be reached, studied, and understood on its own terms.</p>
</article>
<div class="why-timeline-spine"><span class="why-timeline-dot"></span></div>
<aside class="why-timeline-aside">
<p class="why-timeline-aside__text">PenumbraOS exists because there was no other serious path forward. It opens the internals, repairs the missing services, and gives developers a way to make the original software useful again.</p>
</aside>
</div>

<div class="why-timeline-row why-timeline-row--card-right">
<aside class="why-timeline-aside">
<p class="why-timeline-aside__text">Across months of attempts, the picture finally held together long enough to trust on hardware again. Speech, connectivity, and core flows returned without asking anyone to pretend the old cloud was still on the other end of the line.</p>
</aside>
<div class="why-timeline-spine"><span class="why-timeline-dot"></span></div>
<article class="why-tl-card">
<p class="why-tl-card__meta">April 10, 2026</p>
<h3 class="why-tl-card__title">The breakthrough</h3>
<p class="why-tl-card__body">The final barrier fell. What had been fragments of research became a working path back to something familiar: an Ai Pin that could respond, connect, and feel alive again.</p>
</article>
</div>

<div class="why-timeline-row why-timeline-row--card-left">
<article class="why-tl-card why-tl-card--release">
<p class="why-tl-card__meta">May 1, 2026</p>
<h3 class="why-tl-card__title">PenumbraOS arrived</h3>
<p class="why-tl-card__body">The work became something anyone could use. PenumbraOS gave the community a way to bring the Ai Pin back from the edge, and turned abandoned hardware into a platform with a second life.</p>
</article>
<div class="why-timeline-spine"><span class="why-timeline-dot"></span></div>
<aside class="why-timeline-aside">
<p class="why-timeline-aside__text">Shipping meant installers, docs, and source out in the open, with bug reports and pull requests landing from people who still had a Pin in a drawer and decided it was worth another try.</p>
</aside>
</div>
